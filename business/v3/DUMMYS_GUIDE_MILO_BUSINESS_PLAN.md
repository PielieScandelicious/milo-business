## One-Liner Analogies for Common Questions

- **"What's TAM vs SAM vs SOM?"**
  - TAM is the entire pizza market. SAM is pizza shops in Belgium. SOM is the customers who'll actually buy from YOUR shop this year.

- **"What's LTV:CAC?"**
  - If you spend $10 on a dating app and end up married (getting $40 worth of happiness), your LTV:CAC is 4:1. We're at 4.1:1.

- **"What's churn?"**
  - Churn is the "leaky bucket" - 4% monthly means 4 people leave for every 100 you have. We need to keep filling the bucket faster than it drains.

- **"What's gross margin vs operating margin?"**
  - Gross margin is profit after making the product. Operating margin is profit after running the whole company. Like: selling lemonade for $1 that costs $0.20 in ingredients (80% gross margin), but you also paid $0.30 for the stand permit (50% operating margin).

- **"Why is B2B important if B2C is 62%?"**
  - B2B is like the cherry on top that makes the sundae profitable. B2C is the ice cream (most volume), but B2B is pure profit cream at 78% margins.

- **"What makes MILO different?"**
  - Imagine if Mint, MyFitnessPal, and a receipt scanner had a baby powered by ChatGPT. That's MILO. No one else has combined all four.
