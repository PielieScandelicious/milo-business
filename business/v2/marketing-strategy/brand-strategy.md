## Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement

> **For** health-conscious consumers who want to understand their spending,
> **MILO** is the receipt scanning app
> **that** transforms purchase data into actionable wellness insights
> **unlike** cashback apps that only offer rewards,
> **because** we believe understanding your spending patterns is more valuable than collecting points.

## Competitive Differentiation

### vs. Cashback Apps (Fetch, Ibotta)

| Dimension | Cashback Apps | MILO |
|:----------|:--------------|:-----|
| Value proposition | Earn rewards | Gain insights |
| User data use | Sold to advertisers | User-controlled |
| Health focus | None | Core feature |
| Privacy | Opaque | Transparent |

### Brand Message

> "Unlike cashback apps that pay pennies for your data, MILO gives you valuable insights while keeping your data under your control."

---

### vs. Finance Apps (Mint, YNAB)

| Dimension | Finance Apps | MILO |
|:----------|:-------------|:-----|
| Data entry | Bank sync | Receipt scan |
| Item level | No | Yes |
| Health insights | No | Yes |
| User experience | Complex | Simple |

### Brand Message

> "MILO shows you what you bought, not just what you spent. See the health impact of every purchase."

## Brand Guidelines Summary

### Do's

- Use consistent green (#34C759) for primary actions
- Keep messaging focused on user benefits
- Emphasize privacy and control
- Use friendly, encouraging language
- Show real value (insights), not abstract benefits

### Don'ts

- Use technical jargon (AI, ML, OCR) in consumer messaging
- Compare directly to competitors by name
- Make health claims (medical advice)
- Use dark patterns in UI or marketing
- Over-promise on accuracy or results
