Milo Data Products

The core B2B revenue engine. Milo collects receipt-level grocery data from Belgian consumers
(items, brands, prices, stores, categories, Nutri-Scores, timestamps). This data is aggregated,
anonymized, and packaged into data products sold to FMCG brands, retailers, research agencies,
and financial services firms.

USP: The only affordable, cross-retailer consumer panel in Belgium.
- YouGov/GfK panel: 6,000 Belgian households, EUR 100K+/year contracts. Only top brands can afford it.
- Colruyt Insights: 3M households but Colruyt-only (blind to 74% of the market).
- Delhaize Enlight+: Delhaize-only (blind to 77% of the market).
- Carrefour Links: Carrefour-only (blind to 82% of the market).
- Milo: Cross-retailer. Full basket. Affordable. GDPR-compliant first-party data.

Comparable companies:
- NielsenIQ Byzzer (USA): SMB data platform, $4,950-$10,000/year, 15+ report types
- Circana Liquid Data Go: 20 curated reports for SMBs, ~$5K-$15K/year
- Ibotta (USA): $367M revenue in 2024 (87% from redemption commissions + data licensing)
- Fetch Rewards (USA): 11M receipts/day, $1B valuation, data licensing to 300+ brands
- Foxintelligence (France): 600K e-receipt panel, acquired by NielsenIQ
- Shopmium (France/UK/Belgium): receipt scanning, data partnership with Nielsen


--- DATA PRODUCT #1: Milo Monthly Datamart (Foundation Product) ---

What: Pre-aggregated monthly table. One row = one brand x one store x one category x one month.
Grain: brand + store + granular_category + month
Metrics per row: unique_buyers, panel_size, penetration_pct, purchase_frequency,
avg_spend_per_buyer, total_spend, total_units, avg_unit_price, avg_price_per_unit_measure,
category_share_pct, premium_spend_pct, discount_pct, avg_health_score, avg_basket_spend

Dimensions: Time (month/quarter/year), Category (8 groups > 31 parent > ~200 granular),
Store (retailer group > chain > type), Brand (name + private_label flag)

Delivery: CSV/Excel file, monthly. Clients filter and pivot in Excel or their own BI tools.
Target: SMB food brands who need simple, spreadsheet-friendly data.
Price: EUR 200-600/month (EUR 2,400-7,200/year)


--- DATA PRODUCT #2: Milo Brand Scorecard ---

What: A simple, visual one-page brand performance report card.
Composite score across 4 dimensions (like NielsenIQ Byzzer's Brand Score):
  1. Market Position (category share, penetration vs. category average)
  2. Pricing Health (price index vs. category, price gap vs. private label)
  3. Loyalty Strength (repeat rate, purchase frequency, buyer retention)
  4. Promotional Efficiency (promo share, lift vs. non-promo, discount depth)

Each dimension scored 0-100 with trend arrows (up/down/flat vs. prior period).
Benchmarked against category average and top 3 competitors.

Delivery: PDF/PowerPoint, 1-2 pages. Designed to be presentation-ready for retailer meetings.
Updated monthly.
Target: SMB brands who need to pitch retailers (Colruyt, Delhaize, Carrefour) with data.
Price: EUR 100-300/month (EUR 1,200-3,600/year)

Example use case: A Belgian artisanal cheese maker walks into Colruyt's category buyer meeting
with a Milo Brand Scorecard showing: "Premium hard cheese penetration at Colruyt is 14% but
growing +2pt/quarter. Private label holds 39%, Barilla equivalent 21%. There's a clear gap
for a Belgian artisanal brand at EUR 4-5."

--- DATA PRODUCT #4: Cross-Retailer Price & Promotion Monitor ---

What: Tracks ACTUAL prices paid (not shelf prices) and promotional activity across retailers.
Unique because receipt data captures what consumers actually pay after discounts.

Key metrics per brand/category/retailer:
- Average price paid
- Average promoted price vs. regular price
- Promotional depth (% discount)
- % of volume sold on promotion
- Price index vs. category average
- Cross-retailer price comparison (Colruyt vs. Delhaize vs. Lidl vs. Aldi vs. Carrefour)
- Promotional calendar detection (when do promos happen)

Delivery: Monthly dashboard + weekly data feed.
Target: Brand pricing teams, revenue growth management (RGM) teams, key account managers.
Price: EUR 300-800/month (EUR 3,600-9,600/year)

Unique value: Colruyt guarantees "lowest price" — this data can verify whether that holds
at the actual transaction level, and how competitors respond with promotions.


--- DATA PRODUCT #5: Private Label Monitor ---

What: Dedicated tracker for private label dynamics — the defining feature of Belgian grocery
(Belgium has ~40% private label share, one of the highest in Europe).

Tracks:
- PL share by category by retailer (Boni at Colruyt vs. 365 at Delhaize vs. Carrefour brand)
- PL share evolution over time (monthly trend)
- Price gap between PL and equivalent national brands
- Switching behavior: consumers moving from branded to PL (and vice versa)
- PL new product detection
- PL penetration by shopper segment (deal-seekers vs. brand-loyalists)

Delivery: Monthly dashboard + quarterly deep-dive report.
Target: National brand manufacturers (defensive intelligence), retailers' PL teams
(competitive benchmarking), private label contract manufacturers.
Price: EUR 400-1,000/month (EUR 4,800-12,000/year)


--- DATA PRODUCT #6: Shopper Segmentation & Demographics ---

What: Behavioral shopper segments derived from actual purchase data + app profile demographics.
Segments created from receipt patterns:

  - Deal Seekers: >50% of basket on promo, high brand switching for deals
  - Brand Loyalists: >70% repeat rate for core brands, low price sensitivity
  - Premium Shoppers: over-index on organic/premium/specialty, higher basket value
  - Private Label Champions: >60% PL share in basket
  - Health-Conscious / Bio Shoppers: over-index on Nutri-Score A/B, organic, plant-based
  - Budget Constrained: small baskets, high PL, strong promo response, discounter-heavy
  - Young Families: baby products, kids snacks, large weekend baskets

Each segment profiled by: demographics (age, household size, postcode/region),
retailer preference, basket composition, price sensitivity, top categories.

Delivery: Segment profiles (PDF), raw segment flags in datamart, quarterly updates.
Target: Brand marketing teams, media agencies, retailer CRM teams.
Price: EUR 500-1,500/month (EUR 6,000-18,000/year)

Enhanced version: Combine receipt data with in-app micro-surveys (2-3 questions per month)
to add attitudinal data (similar to YouGov's FMCG Shopper Types model).


--- DATA PRODUCT #7: Cross-Shopping & Retailer Loyalty Analysis ---

What: THE unique product that only a cross-retailer panel can provide.
Retailers cannot see this from their own loyalty data. Brands cannot see this from single-
retailer platforms.

Key metrics:
- Store loyalty score (% of total grocery spend at primary store)
- Retailer repertoire size (avg number of stores shopped per month)
- Cross-shopping matrix (% overlap between retailer pairs)
  Example: "35% of Colruyt shoppers also shop at Lidl. 67% of Boni buyers also shop at
  Delhaize, and 54% of those buy 365 products there."
- Category-specific cross-shopping (buys fresh at Colruyt, household at Action)
- Channel migration tracking (hypermarket to discounter, offline to online)
- Customer leakage analysis (for retailers: where are your customers also spending?)

Delivery: Quarterly report + interactive dashboard.
Target: Retailers (will pay premium — EUR 2,500-8,000/month), brand key account teams.
Price: EUR 1,000-3,000/month for brands (EUR 12,000-36,000/year)
       EUR 2,500-8,000/month for retailers (EUR 30,000-96,000/year)

--- COMPETITIVE MOAT ---

1. Cross-retailer view: Colruyt, Delhaize, Carrefour, Lidl, Aldi all in one dataset.
   Retailers will NEVER share cross-retailer data. This gap is permanent.
2. Actual prices paid: Not shelf prices. What consumers actually paid after discounts.
3. Health scoring: Nutri-Score on every item. No panel provider does this.
4. Full-basket context: Basket affinity and cross-purchase patterns.
5. Belgium-specific: Global players lack depth in small markets. Milo = THE Belgian specialist.
6. SMB-accessible pricing: 10-100x cheaper than YouGov/GfK for the most-valued KPIs.
7. Consumer-permissioned: First-party, GDPR-compliant consent-based data.
8. Data compounds: Historical data becomes more valuable every month. First-mover advantage.
9. Receipt + survey hybrid: Can add attitudinal data via in-app micro-surveys (YouGov model).
