# Will FMCG Brands Pay For Your Dataset? Market Validation

## The FMCG "Data Blindspot" — Why Brands Are Desperate

FMCG brands have a fundamental problem: **they don't know who buys their products**. They sell through retailers (Colruyt, Delhaize, Carrefour) and have zero direct consumer relationship. As one analysis puts it: "before, their products were just on the shelves and they didn't know who was buying them."

This is so painful that PepsiCo and others invest in unprofitable D2C channels specifically to get consumer data — not to make money on D2C sales. They're willing to **lose money** just to understand their shoppers.

That's how valuable consumer purchase data is.

---

## Who Actually Buys Panel Data — And Who Can't Afford It

### The buyers (Nielsen/GfK customers)

| Buyer Type | Role | What They Need | Budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Large multinational** (Unilever, Nestle, P&G) | Category Manager, Shopper Insights Manager | Full panel: penetration, loyalty, switching, demographics by category | 300K-1M+/year |
| **Large national brand** (Lotus, Cote d'Or, Devos-Lemmens) | Brand Manager, Trade Marketing | Brand performance vs competitors, retailer negotiations | 100K-300K/year |
| **Mid-size brand** | Marketing Director (wears many hats) | "How am I performing vs private label?" Basic penetration & share | 20K-100K/year |
| **Retailers** (Colruyt, Delhaize) | Category Buyer, Commercial Director | They typically get panel data for free/exchange | 0 (data exchange) |

### The priced-out majority

Belgium has **219,390 FMCG companies**. Of those:

- Maybe **50-100** can afford full Nielsen/GfK subscriptions (100K+)
- Maybe **200-500** can afford Byzzer-level access (5K-20K)
- **218,000+** have **zero access to consumer panel data**

NielsenIQ essentially confirmed this gap when they launched Byzzer: *"Data inequality isn't new to retail. Emerging brands are bootstrapping their way to growth when it comes to data because there has never been an opportunity to harness the insights available to larger competitors."* The CPG market does over $900B in annual retail sales, and SMBs account for **$500B of that** — half the market is flying blind.

## Does It Matter That Your Data Isn't SKU-Level?

**No. Here's why:**

The decisions FMCG brands actually make with panel data are almost all at **category + brand level**, not SKU level:

| Decision | Level Needed | Your Data Covers It? |
|---|---|---|
| "Should we launch a new yoghurt line in Belgium?" | Category penetration + growth trends | **Yes** |
| "Are we losing share to private label?" | Brand share within category | **Yes** |
| "Which retailer should we focus our trade spend on?" | Brand performance by retailer | **Yes** |
| "Is our promotion working?" | Purchase frequency + discount tracking | **Yes** |
| "What's the profile of our typical buyer?" | Category + demographics | **Yes (with demographic data)** |
| "Are Jupiler drinkers switching to Cara pils?" | Brand switching within beer | **Yes** |
| "How does our 4x125g pack perform vs 8x125g?" | SKU-level variant comparison | **No** |

That last row — SKU-level pack size analysis — is the one thing you can't do. But it's a niche use case. **90% of the business questions are answered at category + brand level.**

## Your Real Customers: 5 Segments

### Segment 1: Belgian SMB food producers (LARGEST opportunity)
- **Who**: The 1000s of Belgian food producers — artisanal cheese makers, local breweries, sauces, spreads, chocolates, charcuterie
- **Current data access**: None. Zero. They pitch retailers with zero market data.
- **What they'd pay for**: "How big is the artisanal cheese category at Colruyt vs Delhaize? Is it growing? What price point works?"
- **Price sensitivity**: 2K-8K/year is their sweet spot
- **Why they'd buy from you**: Nielsen doesn't even know they exist. Byzzer is US-focused. You're local, Belgian, affordable.

### Segment 2: Mid-size Belgian/European brands
- **Who**: Lotus, Devos-Lemmens, Leonidas, Cote d'Or (Mondelez BE), La William, Imperial Meat Products
- **Current data access**: Probably basic Nielsen, maybe just Byzzer tier
- **What they'd pay for**: A cheaper second opinion. Cross-retailer shopper behavior. Competitive intel on private label.
- **Price sensitivity**: 15K-50K/year
- **Why they'd buy from you**: Supplement (not replace) their Nielsen subscription with unique receipt-based shopper insights

### Segment 3: Private label / retailer brands
- **Who**: Colruyt (Boni, Everyday), Delhaize (365), Carrefour (own brand)
- **Current data access**: They have their OWN POS data, but only for THEIR stores
- **What they'd pay for**: **Cross-retailer** visibility — "how does our Boni yoghurt compete vs 365 at Delhaize?" — something their own POS can NEVER tell them
- **Price sensitivity**: 30K-100K/year
- **Why they'd buy from you**: This is data they literally cannot get anywhere else. Nielsen won't show Colruyt what happens at Delhaize at this level.

### Segment 4: Market research agencies and consultants
- **Who**: The dozens of Belgian/European research firms that advise FMCG brands
- **Current data access**: They resell Nielsen or do custom studies (expensive)
- **What they'd pay for**: A cheaper data source they can analyze and repackage for their clients
- **Price sensitivity**: 10K-30K/year for raw data access
- **Why they'd buy from you**: Better margins for them vs buying Nielsen

### Segment 5: Investors, hedge funds, alternative data buyers
- **Who**: Financial firms tracking Belgian consumer spending
- **Current data access**: Credit card transaction data (no item level)
- **What they'd pay for**: Real-time Belgian consumer spending by category, before official stats are published
- **Price sensitivity**: 20K-80K/year
- **Why they'd buy from you**: Unique dataset, real-time, granular

## Proof the Model Works: Shopmium

Shopmium is a receipt scanning cashback app operating in **France, UK, and Belgium**. Their business model is almost exactly what you're describing:

1. Consumers scan receipts -> get cashback offers
2. Shopmium collects purchase data from receipts
3. They partnered with Nielsen to sell this data
4. FMCG brands pay for: consumer behavior data, audience segments, targeted promotions
5. Nielsen models and scales the Shopmium data for programmatic advertising activation

Shopmium has 10M+ downloads and FMCG brands pay them for 500+ buyer segments. **They proved that receipt-level data (not SKU-level, not barcode-level) is valuable enough for Nielsen itself to partner with them.**

However, Shopmium's data is **biased** — users only scan receipts when claiming cashback offers. Your users scan ALL receipts, giving you unbiased, full-basket data.

## How to Validate Demand Before Building

Don't build the data product first. Validate with these steps:

### 1. Talk to 10-15 potential buyers (2-3 weeks)
- Find Belgian food producers at [food.be](https://www.food.be/companies) or [The Belgian Food Company](https://belgianfoodcompany.be/)
- Find brand managers on LinkedIn (search "brand manager FMCG Belgium")
- Ask them: "How do you currently understand your shoppers? Do you use Nielsen/GfK? If not, why? What would you pay for X?"
- You're not selling yet — you're learning

### 2. Create a sample insights report (1 week)
- Pick a category (e.g., yoghurt, beer, or chips)
- Using your REAL data from 5,000 users, generate:
  - Category penetration (% of your users buying yoghurt)
  - Brand share within yoghurt (Danone vs Alpro vs private label)
  - Average price paid by brand
  - Purchase frequency
  - Which stores capture the most yoghurt spend
- Make it a clean PDF. This is your sales tool.

### 3. Show it to potential buyers and ask "would you pay X/month for this, updated monthly?"
- If 3 out of 10 say yes -> you have a business
- If they say "this is interesting but I need X" -> you learn exactly what to build

### 4. Check if Datarade would list you
- [Datarade](https://datarade.ai/data-categories/fmcg-data) is a marketplace for data products. Belgian FMCG receipt data is a niche they'd likely accept. Listing is free and brings inbound buyers.
