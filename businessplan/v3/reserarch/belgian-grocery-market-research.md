## 2. Belgian Consumer Panel / Market Research Industry

### Key Players

#### YouGov Consumer Panel (ex-GfK)
- **What**: ~6,000 households scanning barcodes of all FMCG purchases
- **Coverage**: Cross-retailer (Colruyt, Delhaize, Carrefour, Aldi, Lidl, Albert Heijn)
- **Methodology**: Households use in-home scanners to record all purchases from any outlet
- **Scale**: Part of a 100,000+ household panel across 18 European countries
- **Pricing**: Six-figure annual contracts (EUR 100,000+). Average contract length is 3 years. Two-thirds of revenues are recurring.
- **History**: GfK sold its European Consumer Panel business to YouGov in 2023. Before that, GfK was acquired by NielsenIQ in July 2022.
- **Limitation**: Only accessible to top 50-100 brands in Belgium

Sources: [YouGov CPS GfK Acquisition](https://business.yougov.com/company/consumer-panel-services-cps-gfk-yougov-acquisition), [GfK Panel Sale to YouGov](https://www.research-live.com/article/news/gfk-sells-european-consumer-panel-business-to-yougov/id/5121233)

#### NielsenIQ (NIQ)
- **What**: Retail measurement data + consumer panel services globally
- **Belgium role**: Retail Spend Barometer tracking FMCG market trends
- **Panel scale**: Tracks 5.6M consumers across 53 countries globally
- **Belgium-specific**: In 2021, GfK Belgium and Daltix enriched the Belgian consumer panel with promotional information at individual purchase level
- **Affordable product**: NIQ's self-service tool Byzzer exists but is US-only. No equivalent in Belgium/Europe.
- **Pricing**: Not publicly disclosed; enterprise-level pricing, typically EUR 50K-200K+ per year depending on scope

Sources: [NIQ Consumer Panel](https://nielseniq.com/global/en/info/consumer-panel/), [NIQ Belgium Retail Spend Barometer](https://nielseniq.com/global/en/landing-page/retail-spend-barometer-belgium/)

#### Kantar Worldpanel
- **What**: Continuous household purchase panel tracking FMCG
- **Scale**: 750,000+ consumers tracked worldwide
- **Services**: Market share tracking, brand penetration, purchase frequency, loyalty metrics
- **Belgium coverage**: Included in Europanel (non-exclusive collaboration between Kantar, YouGov, and other partners)
- **Belgium e-commerce insight**: Kantar Belgium reported online grocery market grew 30%

Sources: [Kantar Consumer Goods Panel](https://www.kantar.com/expertise/consumer-shopper-retail/consumer-panels/consumer-goods-panel), [Kantar Belgium E-commerce](https://kantar.be/e-commerce_grocery_market_has_grown), [Europanel Partners](https://europanel.com/global-coverage/our-partners/)

### How Consumer Panels Work

1. **Recruitment**: A representative sample of households is recruited (e.g., 6,000 in Belgium for YouGov)
2. **Data collection**: Panelists scan barcodes of every FMCG product purchased, or scan full receipts
3. **Enrichment**: Data is matched to product databases, tagged with promotions, categories, and pricing
4. **Output**: Monthly/weekly reports with KPIs like penetration, frequency, basket size, spend per buyer, market share
5. **Delivery**: Dashboards, flat files, or API access
6. **Incentives**: Panelists receive points/rewards (e.g., NielsenIQ Consumer Panel app rewards users for scanning)

### Typical Pricing for Consumer Panel Data (Estimates)

| Tier | Annual Cost (EUR) | Typical Client |
|---|---|---|
| Full cross-retailer panel subscription | EUR 100,000-250,000+ | Top 50 FMCG brands (Unilever, Nestle, PepsiCo) |
| Single-retailer data (Colruyt Insights, MMD) | EUR 10,000-50,000 | Mid-size brands, regional players |
| Ad-hoc studies / custom reports | EUR 5,000-20,000 | Smaller brands, occasional needs |
| Self-service data tools (e.g., Byzzer in US) | USD 500-2,000/month | SMBs — but this does NOT exist in Belgium |

**Key gap**: There is no affordable, self-service consumer panel data tool in Belgium or Europe for SMBs. The 3,500+ smaller food producers have zero access to cross-retailer consumer data.

## 4. Belgian Food Price Inflation

### Timeline

| Period | Food Inflation (YoY) | Overall CPI |
|---|---|---|
| 2022 (peak) | ~12-15% | 10.3% |
| 2023 | ~8-10% | 2.3% |
| December 2025 | 2.81% | 2.06% |
| November 2025 | 3.07% | 2.4% |
| January 2026 | 0.44% | 1.1% (lowest since Nov 2023) |

### Key Takeaways

- Food inflation has **moderated significantly** from the 2022 peak (12-15%) to near-zero (0.44% in January 2026)
- The CPI in Belgium stood at ~132 points in 2024, indicating a 31.68% increase in basket prices since the 2013 base year
- As prices stabilized in 2024, pressure on purchasing power eased, leading to consumers becoming more willing to buy online again
- **Business plan implication**: Consumers remain highly price-sensitive after years of inflation. A tool that helps them track and compare grocery spending addresses a real pain point.

Sources: [Trading Economics Belgium Food Inflation](https://tradingeconomics.com/belgium/food-inflation), [Statbel Consumer Price Index](https://statbel.fgov.be/en/themes/consumer-prices/consumer-price-index)

## 7. Data Privacy & GDPR Considerations

### Regulatory Framework

- **Primary regulation**: GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation, EU 2016/679)
- **Belgian implementation**: Law of 30 July 2018 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data
- **Supervisory authority**: Autorite de Protection des Donnees (APD) / Gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit (GBA)

### Key GDPR Principles for Consumer Panel / Receipt Data

1. **Lawfulness**: Need a valid legal basis — for consumer panels, this is typically **explicit consent** (Art. 6(1)(a))
2. **Data minimization**: Only collect data that is strictly necessary. The Belgian DPA fined a merchant EUR 10,000 for requiring electronic ID cards for loyalty cards (excessive data collection)
3. **Purpose limitation**: Data collected for one purpose cannot be repurposed without new consent
4. **Transparency**: Must clearly inform users what data is collected, how it is used, who receives it
5. **Right to erasure**: Users must be able to delete their data at any time
6. **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**: Required for large-scale processing of consumer data

### Belgian DPA Enforcement — Recent Fines

| Year | Fine (EUR) | Violation |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 174,640 | Data broker — unlawful processing, failure to disclose sources |
| 2024 | 172,431 | Failure to erase data used in direct marketing |
| 2024 | 40,000/day | RTL Belgium — non-compliant cookie banners |
| 2024 | 8,000 | Unsolicited commercial email without opt-out |
| 2025 | 20,000 | B2B data broker — unlawful email collection |
| Highest ever | 600,000 | Google Belgium — right to be forgotten |

### Practical Implications for a Receipt-Scanning App

- **Consent mechanism**: Must obtain explicit, informed, freely given consent before processing receipt data
- **Anonymization/Pseudonymization**: Aggregated data products sold to B2B clients should be fully anonymized (no individual-level data)
- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)**: Required with any third-party processors (cloud providers, AI services like Google Gemini for OCR)
- **Loyalty card precedent**: Belgian DPA has specifically ruled on loyalty card data practices — requiring minimal data collection and genuine choice
- **Cross-border transfers**: If using US cloud services, must comply with EU-US Data Privacy Framework
- **Children's data**: Extra protections under GDPR for minors (parental consent required under 16, or under 13 in some member states)

Sources: [White & Case GDPR Belgium](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-our-thinking/gdpr-guide-national-implementation-belgium), [Chambers Data Protection Belgium 2025](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/data-protection-privacy-2025/belgium), [GDPRhub Belgian DPA Decisions](https://gdprhub.eu/APD/GBA_(Belgium)), [Crowell Belgian DPA Loyalty Card](https://www.crowelldatalaw.com/2019/10/belgian-data-protection-authority-finds-merchant-violated-gdpr-by-requiring-customers-to-provide-electronic-id-to-receive-loyalty-card/)
