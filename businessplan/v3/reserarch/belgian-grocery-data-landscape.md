# The Belgian Grocery Data Landscape: Who Has What

## The Incumbents

There are **5 data sources** already operating in Belgium, but each has a critical limitation that creates your opportunity:

### 1. YouGov Consumer Panel (ex-GfK)
- **What**: 6,000 households scanning barcodes of all FMCG purchases
- **Coverage**: Cross-retailer (Colruyt, Delhaize, Carrefour, Aldi, Lidl, AH)
- **Strength**: The gold standard — 85 KPIs, 2.1M SKUs, 60 years of methodology
- **Limitation**: Expensive (six figures). Only the top ~50-100 brands in Belgium can afford it
- **Your gap**: The 218,000+ companies that can't afford this

### 2. Colruyt Group Insights
- **What**: ~3 million households, 150M transactions/year from Xtra loyalty card
- **Coverage**: **Only Colruyt, OKay, and Spar** — their own stores
- **Strength**: Massive dataset, near real-time, self-service dashboards
- **Limitation**: **Cannot see what happens at Delhaize, Carrefour, Aldi, or Lidl.** A brand cannot understand cross-retailer behavior.
- **Your gap**: You see ALL stores. A brand using Colruyt Insights has zero visibility into 74% of the market.
- **Link**: [insights.colruytgroup.com](https://insights.colruytgroup.com/en)

### 3. Media Marketing Delhaize (MMD) / Enlight+
- **What**: 3M+ SuperPlus loyalty profiles, transactional data
- **Coverage**: **Only Delhaize stores**
- **Strength**: Deep shopper profiles, retail media activation (ads on Delhaize.be, in-store screens)
- **Limitation**: **Only Delhaize.** Same blind spot as Colruyt Insights but for the other side.
- **Clients**: Nestle, PepsiCo, Unilever, Coca-Cola — the big boys. Sells advertising + data together.
- **Your gap**: Again, cross-retailer visibility.
- **Link**: [mediamarketingdelhaize.be](https://www.mediamarketingdelhaize.be/)

### 4. Carrefour Links Belgium (via LiveRamp clean room)
- **What**: 2.4M loyalty card consumers, data clean room
- **Coverage**: **Only Carrefour**
- **Clients in Belgium**: Duvel, Lotus, Nestle, FrieslandCampina
- **Limitation**: Same single-retailer blindspot. And it's a clean room — brands can't export raw data, only run queries.
- **Your gap**: Cross-retailer, and you can provide actual datasets.

### 5. Shopmium Belgium
- **What**: Cashback app, 2,500+ brand offers, receipt scanning
- **Coverage**: Cross-retailer (Colruyt, Delhaize, Carrefour, Aldi, Lidl)
- **Strength**: Already has Belgian users, partnered with Nielsen for data activation
- **Limitation**: **Shopmium is a cashback app, not a panel.** Users only scan receipts when they use a Shopmium offer. They DON'T scan every receipt. This means the data is heavily biased toward promoted products — you only see purchases where the user claimed a deal. No reliable penetration, frequency, or basket data.
- **Belgian scale**: Unknown, but their 8M+ users are global (mainly UK + France). Belgium is likely a small fraction — probably tens of thousands at best, not hundreds of thousands.
- **Your gap**: Your 5,000 users send ALL receipts, not just promo receipts. Your data represents actual shopping behavior, not coupon-clipping behavior.
- **Link**: [shopmium.com/be-nl](https://www.shopmium.com/be-nl)

### 6. PingPrice Belgium
- **What**: Price comparison app, 1M+ prices/day across 75 stores
- **Coverage**: Delhaize, Colruyt, Carrefour, AH, Cora
- **Strength**: Great pricing data, B2B API available
- **Limitation**: **Price data only, no purchase behavior.** They know what things cost, not who buys what or how often.
- **Your gap**: You have actual purchase data, not just shelf prices.
- **Link**: [pingprice.be](https://www.pingprice.be/en/)

## Your Unique Position

The Belgian market has a very specific structural gap:

**Retailers monetize their OWN data** (Colruyt Insights, Delhaize MMD, Carrefour Links) but they will **never share cross-retailer data**. Colruyt will never tell a brand what happens at Delhaize. Delhaize will never reveal shopper behavior at Colruyt. This is a fundamental competitive dynamic that will never change.

**YouGov (ex-GfK) is the only cross-retailer panel**, but it's expensive and caters to large multinationals.

**Shopmium has biased data** — only promo-triggered scans, not full shopping behavior.

**You are the only affordable, cross-retailer, full-basket consumer panel in Belgium.**

This is not a marginal advantage. For any FMCG brand that wants to understand:
- "When my customer shops at Colruyt, what do they buy at Delhaize?"
- "Is Boni volle melk taking share from my brand at Colruyt while my brand grows at Delhaize?"
- "Which retailer has the highest penetration for my category?"

...they currently have **two options**: pay YouGov 100K+, or guess. You'd be option three.

---

## The Real Competition Risk

Your risk isn't Shopmium (biased data, not a panel) or the retailers (single-store silos). Your risk is:

1. **YouGov dropping their price** — They could launch a Byzzer-like self-service tier for Belgium. But they haven't in 60 years of operating there, so unlikely short-term.

2. **A retailer opening up** — Colruyt could theoretically sell cross-retailer data if they partnered with Delhaize. But they're direct competitors — this will never happen.

3. **Another receipt app scaling in Belgium** — This is the real risk. Someone else building what you have. But you have a 5,000-user head start and 6 months of data. First-mover advantage in panel data is enormous because historical data compounds in value.

---

## What To Do Next

1. **Build that sample insights report now** — pick beer or yoghurt, show cross-retailer brand share from your actual data
2. **Talk to 5-10 Belgian food producers** — show them something they've literally never seen before (their category at Colruyt vs Delhaize vs Aldi)
3. **Position yourself as "the cross-retailer alternative"** — not competing with YouGov on methodology, but filling the gap that retailer data silos cannot
